Platform Safety · Resident Experience
When things
go wrong.
Community platforms attract bad actors. Dwlr handles this at the platform layer first — so the operator isn't the first line of defense, and the victim isn't left waiting.
1
Resident taps "Report" — anonymous, one tap
Categories: harassment, inappropriate content, threatening behavior, spam. The reporter is never identified to the reported user. No exceptions.
2
AI triage routes by severity — immediately
Low → auto-warning. Medium → content hidden, human review queued. High → immediate removal, operator alerted, escalation pathway triggered.
3
Platform acts before the operator is notified
Offending content is removed or hidden instantly at Medium and High. The victim is protected before the operator picks up their phone.
4
Reporter gets a resolution update
"We've reviewed your report and taken action." The reporter knows something happened — without exposing what action was taken or compromising confidentiality.
Severity tiers
Low — Advisory
Spam, minor rudeness. Auto-warning sent.
Medium — Action
Harassment, inappropriate. Hidden + reviewed.
High — Escalate
Threats, safety risk. Removed instantly + operator alerted.
Privacy protected
Reporter identity is never revealed to the reported user — at any severity level. Confidentiality is non-negotiable.

Platform Safety · Operator Engagement
The escalation ladder.
Who acts at each rung.
Dwlr handles the platform layer. The operator owns the real-world response. Neither steps into the other's lane.
1
Platform auto-action — Dwlr handles entirely
Content removed or hidden. Warning sent. Reporter notified. Operator not involved — no action required below Medium.
2
Community manager notified — advisory only
Alex gets a dashboard alert: "Medium report resolved. Awareness only." She can monitor the flagged unit's patterns. No content shown. No names disclosed.
3
Community manager action required
High-severity alert pushed to Alex in real time. She sees: unit number, report category, platform action taken. She decides the real-world response — welfare check, lease conversation, formal warning. Dwlr gives the signal; she owns the action.
4
Property management + legal — escalate
Repeated offenses or credible threats. Dwlr provides a timestamped incident log to support lease termination or law enforcement referral. Dwlr does not contact authorities directly — that decision belongs to the operator and their legal team.
