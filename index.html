Resident Experience
← The engagement loop that protects renewals
The building itself
becomes the venue.
Two event streams live in a single resident feed. Resident-created micro-events keep the community alive daily. Operator-seeded anchors give you control of the calendar. When life gets busy and a resident goes quiet — Vivio Life notices first.
1
Resident-created micro-events
Jordan posts a study session — the platform pushes it to Study-tagged residents. Three RSVP. No staff involvement, no flyer. Every attendee's pledge ("I'll bring snacks") is publicly visible, cutting no-shows from 50% to ~20%.
2
Operator-seeded anchor events
You post a Rooftop Movie Night in 60 seconds. Vivio suggests the best time based on resident availability data. Targeted to matching vibes first, then building-wide. 22 RSVPs — all flowing to your dashboard in real time.
3
Connections leave the building
Maya and Jordan's coffee run becomes a Saturday ritual. The community is the anchor — the address is just where it started. Residents who build social lives rooted in the building are dramatically less likely to leave it.
The Nudge System — when Maya goes quiet, Vivio notices
Finals week. 9 days since Maya opened the app. She missed Movie Night, didn't reply to Jordan. The platform detects the drop and responds — not with a generic push notification, but something tied to her life specifically.
Contextual, never promotional
"Jordan just posted a study group for Thursday — you're both free." Built from her availability data. Feels like a recommendation, not an alert.
Energy type shapes frequency
Ambivert = warm and spaced. Introvert = fewer, quieter. Extrovert = higher frequency. Residents never feel pushed — just seen.
One tap re-engages → churn signal clears on your dashboard
Maya taps, RSVPs, her engagement score recovers. Your dashboard clears the at-risk signal. This happened 48 days before a renewal conversation would have surfaced it.

How bad actors are handled
Residents can flag inappropriate behavior through an anonymous in-app report. Flagged accounts are reviewed and can be suspended by the operator — without exposing who filed the report. The operator sees only that a flag exists and its category (harassment, spam, misconduct). Identity of the reporter is never surfaced. Community safety is maintained without creating a surveillance dynamic.
